Game Analysis

A page to contain the Game Analysis project for DIGIT-100.

Risk of Rain 2 is a roguelike shooter which takes place on an alien planet, Petrichor V. The main goal of the game is to escape the planet alive. To do so, the player must achieve one of the game's endings, usually through defeating a final boss. On their journey, the player will explore several unique stages, defeat strong monsters and bosses, and collect a vast amount of items to aid them in the game.

The stages in which the player finds themselves are vast and unique to themselves in theme. The size of the stage correlates to how far in the game the stage appears, as later stages become larger to compensate for the player beocming faster. The appearance of each stage is very unique, usually featuring completely different color palletes and layouts.

In order to move between stages, the player must complete a teleporter event. Each teleporter event will spawn a boss for the stage, and must be charged for a minimum of 90 seconds. The player can charge the teleporter by standing inside of the indicated zone.

Some stages are secret, and can only be accessed through special means. The lunar bazaar is a secret shop stage, which can only be accessed by interacting with a newt altar for the cost of a lunar coin. The gilded coast is a secret stage accesed through paying money to an altar of gold.

The moon of Petrichor V serves as the arena for the final boss, Mithrix. To access the moon, the player must activate the primordial teleporter, which replaces the regular teleporter on stage five. Once on the moon, the player must charge four pillars and confront Mithrix. If the player is successful, a rescue ship will land and serve as the final objective to win the game.

The game allows you to choose several characters to play, called survivors. Each survivor offers a different playstyle to shape the gameplay. Some survivors specialize in movement, while others are more positional focused, or have a different strength. To add even more variety, most survivors have alternate skills which can be selected to change the way they play.

Main Game Review

Overview

Risk of Rain 2 is a roguelike third person shooter. The player finds themselves as a survivor amongst an alien planet, Petrichor V. Following a distress call, a ship is sent to investigate. This second ship sends the player to the planet's surface and marks the beginning of the game. To survive, the player battles endless hordes of monsters and explores incredible environments along the way. To help them, the player has access to items which grant them powerful boosts, working off of their own set of usable abilities.

Environment

Risk of Rain 2 contains a good quantity of unique, explorable environments. Each stage is vastly different from one another, usually both in layout and appearance. The functionality of each stage depends on how far the player must progress to reach it. Earlier stages are small, while later stages are large. This size difference is to compensate for the player increasing in speed as they collect items which enhance their movement. Later stages also commonly have a higher level of vertical complexity for this reason as well. The music track for each stage is usually different, with an exception lying in the same song being used for Gilded Coast and Rallypoint Delta. Tne music was created with a few parameters in mind to aid the gameplay. The composer, Chris Christodolou has mentioned in his developer commentaries that the songs needed to be long to accomodate for the player spending a potentially large amount of time on each stage. He explained that the first game needed only to convey the impression that the environments were large, since it was a two dimensional game. Risk of Rain 2, however, is three dimensional. Chris acknowledged this, and made music to accompany environments that actually were this large in size. It's easy to get lost in exploring each stage at first! To progress between stages, the player must complete the teleporter event. (Explained in later section)

Characters

The characters in Risk of Rain 2 are called survivors, and fucntion as choosable options to control the game's playstyle. Each survivor looks and plays very differently. Some survivors attack from a long range, while others prefer to get in close. Some control the area around them with abilities that take hold on the environment around them, while others are durable enough to survive nearly anything thrown at them. They can be astronauts from the ship sent to Petrichor V, or a lizard that spits toxic venom out of its mouth. Point is, there is a lot of variation in the options provided. As far as NPC's, theres only really one option that comes to mind. Newt is the lunar shopkeeper, who oversees the Bazaar Between Time. Newt doesn't really do much themselves, and has no notable interactions other than booting the player out if they decide to hurt Newt.

Monsters

Monsters are the main threat to the player throughout the game. Monsters will continuously spawn in waves on nearly all stages until the teleporter event is completed. There are plenty of monster types which the game can spawn, usually decided by how long the player has been playing for and what stage they are on. Monsters can also spawn as elites. Elite monsters take on a particular color tied to their elite type, and gain a specific buff as well. Blazing elites set the player on fire, and leave a trail of flames where they move. Glacial elites slow the player, and explode into ice on death. Overloading elites attatch an explosive electric orb where they attack, dealing additional damage to the player. Mending elites heal nearby monsters, and drop a healing explosion on death. Gilded elites periodically surround themselves in a barrier, stealing the player's money if hit. Malachite elites disable the player's ability to heal for a time, and periodically shoot out spikes which do the same. Celestine elites create a large field around themselves, making all monsters inside of it become invisible. Twisted elites create a field around themselves, which retaliates damage to the player if a monster inside of it is damaged. Perfected elites will cripple the player on hit, and shoot homing fireballs periodically. Monsters can also be bosses. Bosses are much stronger monsters, usually very large in size. While boss monsters can spawn naturally, they are most often seen as the primary target of a teleporter event. Bosses can also be any of the aforementioned elite types!

Interactables & Items

Items

"Items" form the foundation of how the game is played. Items are collectibles that the player can receive through several means, most common of which being through opening a chest for money that the player collects through killing monsters. Items generally provide passive effect bonuses that make the player stronger in some aspect. Some items allow the player to deal more damage, while others offer a source of healing. Items can be of several different qualities: Common, Uncommon, Legendary, Boss Items, Lunar, Equipment, or Void items. Rarer items typically provide a greater effect, but are harder to come by.

Interactables

Interactables serve several different purposes in Risk of Rain 2. To elaborate from earlier, the teleporter is an interactable found on most stages which serves as the main way to advance the game. On activation, the teleporter spawns a boss which must be defeated. In addition, the teleporter must be charged to 99% as well. This sequence is refered to as the "teleporter event". Chests function as another important interactable. As a list of other interactables:

  • Drones - Repairable allies which cost money to activate.
  • Shrines - Causes special effects determined by the type of shrine.
  • Multishops - Offers a choice between several items, rather than pure chance like with chests.
  • Barrels - Can be opened for money.
  • ETC of similar purposes.

Personal Conclusion

Risk of Rain 2 is a fantastic game I can heavily recommend to anybody with a taste for roguelikes. It's a genre defining entry that shakes the idea of whats possible in an indie title. I've accumulated hundreds of hours in playtime, and will very likely continue to grow that number over time. The game series was originally created by two college students, and saw such success that it has grown into what it is today. Gearbox, the publishing studio, has purchased the IP from Hopoo games. While the future of what's to come of the Risk of Rain franchise is uncertain, I would like to remain hopeful that future entries of this game are possible. As another note, the music for this game is incredible! Cannot stress enough how amazing Chris Christodolou's work is in this game. Look him up on youtube sometime! I've even tried to by the records!
